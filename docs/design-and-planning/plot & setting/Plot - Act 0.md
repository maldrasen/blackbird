### The Lonely Shepard
You start the game as a human male. Your home was in Borr, out in the farmlands. Though most valuable resources come from the dungeon food, wool, wood, and such are still farmed. The farmlands are quite safe and idyllic. People who make farming a career are looked down on though, because they lack the strength that the dungeon gives people. 

> Actually, people who use magic are distrusted, and people who don't delve are disrespected. I think it's more accurate to say that there's a big trust gap between the adventurers and the non-adventurers. Normal people think that adventurers are psychotic power hungry bastards, and they're correct. Adventures think normal people are weak, lazy, and easy to kill if they get in the way. 

Your father was an adventurer, who died when you were young. After his death your mother took what he had earned from delving and bought this farm, abandoning their house in the city. You have an older brother who is to inherit the farm, and another older brother who left for the city to become a delver, but hasn't been heard from again. You have a younger half-brother and two younger half-sisters as well. (People breed and die a lot in this setting. Your mother never got remarried, she just likes to fuck.) 

You've known for a while that it was your intent to become an adventurer. Your family gets some money selling wool and mutton, but could really use more money and your older brother didn't send anything home. So your mother charges you with seeing if you can find out what happened to him, and to send money back home. She tells you where your father's old house in the city is. They still own the house, so you're free to use it, though it's been abandoned for years. Probably make a few jokes about how the sheep are all dripping cum after you'd been watching them.
