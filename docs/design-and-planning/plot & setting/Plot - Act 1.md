# Act 1 : The Fucking New Guy
We need to start the game by giving the player a parcel of land located within Wolgur. They can start with a simple tent or shack and eventually construct a great manor house.
- Where does this land come from? A gift from a relative? The church?

The player quickly learns that he's completely on his own. The houses of Wolgur horde their craftsmen, so there's no town blacksmith, no tavern, etc. For now he only has what he's brought with him.
- There does need to be some kind of pawn shop though, some place that allows independent people to sell their dungeon loot for bad rates. Really just there to take advantage of the factionless delvers.
- The people of the town seem unfriendly and suspicious of outsiders. Anyone new in tows in competition after all.

One of the first friendly faces they meet should be a priestess, seen helping the poor. She's terribly naive, but genuinely concerned for the player. She admonishes him to avoid corruption and will ask him to try and destroy the place, as that is all delvers sacred duty. If he agrees she can offer him some help getting started, maybe a few simple quests. She wouldn't use any resources found in the dungeon, but he can be sent to find people who have gone missing.

Exploring the dungeon is difficult. Though you should probably start out fairly strong, you're still a single human with no healing spells. The start of the game presents a fairly basic loop. Go into the dungeon, kill things, grab scraps of loot to sell for ridiculously low prices, attempt to upgrade your shitty gear.

A single human can probably handle the first floor of the dungeon which only spawns weak enemies. Basic shit like rats, and spiders. The difficulty spikes as they descend down to the second floor where the kobolds and vermen start appearing. Eventually you'll die, (almost die) and we have a scene where you're being fucked by the succubus who rescues you. She offers you power in exchange for your humanity. She has already given you the ability to speak with monsters, because she needed to talk to you. 

When you agree she gives you the ability to gain experience by absorbing the monster's essence when they're killed or fucked, then she fucks you. From now on, when you kill all but one monster in a battle, that final monster might try and convince you not to kill them. You can negotiate with them, having the option to add them to your party.
- You won't want to add a monster every fight though, so monsters could also give you essence; just a quicky, not a full training scene. 
- There should be other valuable things they could give you, though any loot they have you could have just gotten by killing them. Information about the dungeon, maybe knowledge of the floor layout, or traps ahead. Maybe information that would let you ambush the next fight. Perhaps the location of a treasure chest.
- Some monsters could trick you as well, using a negotiation as a chance to backstab you or run away.
- Any of the information they give you could be lies as well, perhaps they're trying to lure you into an ambush.
- We need some kind of skill or spell to detect lies. Detecting lies won't work on deceptive statements though.
- After you've been given information or had sex with them, you could then kill them anyway too. There needs to be some kind of consequence for that kind of behavior though. We need to have some kind of reputation system. If you treat the monsters fairly they're more generous. They'll start out with better attitude towards the player. If you're a psycho they won't trust you, will lie to you more, will be harder to convince to join you. Will need to be a fine balance though. Punishing someone who lied to you should be acceptable, but killing less so. Killing someone who was helpful should be very bad though.
