I'm starting to think about how I want to structure and theme the dungeon beyond the starting kobold/vermen area. My overall idea for the dungeon is an impossible castle like structure. I think I want the dungeon floors to extend upward, rather than down. The entrance of the dungeon can be in the side of a mountain, so having the dungeon proceed upward for a few floors seems logical, though as you climb a hundred floors it's clear that the space the dungeon occupies doesn't make any logical sense. I'll make this clear a few levels up, when some rooms randomly have windows that look out across impossible landscapes. The views that these random windows display become increasingly bizarre the further up you go.
### The Lower Levels 
The lowest levels of the dungeon are the most traditional. Stone walls, areas that might have been prisons or jails; sewer areas, and caves. Havlins will sometimes show up in the lower levels, doing crimes. 
### The Dark City
After you've managed to find a way past the lower levels you'll arrive in a 'city' type area. The city is still clearly entirely indoors, with the roof far overhead. As you proceed higher in the city the level design features more bridges that cross between buildings with the streets below. We'll need a way to adjust the dungeon builder so that it can cluster rooms together in islands that we then connect with bridges. The story of the city should focus on various gangs, fighting for territory, doing crimes. There is a small elf presence here, here to look after the interest of the upper cities. 
##### The Dark City as a Crossroads
The dungeon isn't entirely linear. From the dark city will branch out into a bunch of different areas. There's an obvious entrance to the upper city, but you won't be able to get past the gate until much later. You'll need to visit other areas first.
##### The Cracks
The Dark City doesn't really have boundaries, but the further out you go the more ruined and abandoned they are. You'll need to go through the cracks to reach places like the equin fort or lupin castle. 

This raises a question about how I want to handle the "overnavigation" of the dungeon. Currently I was thinking the dungeon just had levels, and the higher you were determined what kind of theme was going to spawn. However, if the dungeon is going to have branches we need to have like an overall area map that defines what areas connect to other areas. Each 'layer' is still randomized, and perhaps you have to cross through several layers in order to get to a boundary layer where you can transition to a new area.

Just a simplified version of what I'm thinking, but from the Lower Levels `[LL]` you go from floor 1 to floor 4, each floor being randomly generated and themed. The gate to the `[DC]` only generates on level 4 of the lower levels area. From the lower levels you arrive in the center of dark city. From central dark city you can go up into the upper city. Or you can go outwards. The levels in the dark city represent how far away from the city center you are. The cardinal directions have no meaning in the dungeon, but there is always up or down, or inward and outward. Same is true for the upper city which has spires and bridges that cross over the dark city, always spreading outward, or up to the castle. 
### The Upper City
Eventually you'll get past the dark city gate to be allowed entry into the upper city. The upper city is dominated by the elves, living lives of decadence. As immortals, the elves are almost vampire like. The upper city will branch like the lower city does as the elves are involved in all kind of evil shit, human experimentation, meat farming, demon worshiping cults etc. Once you reach the upper city though it becomes clear that the elves are only lower nobility, who are themselves vassals of the gentry to live in the castle, the area above this one.
### The Castle
The dungeon royalty are fae like creatures. While we've seen the nymphs and sylphs and such throughout the dungeon, they originate in the castle where they work as servants mostly. The gentry are strange, cruel, and alien in their interests. 
### The Equin Fort
The equin are used as soldiers throughout the dungeon. They have a very military like society. From the dark city you can get access to an equin fort where they train. It wouldn't make sense for you to be invading their space. If you weren't allowed there they'd gang up and kill you. Perhaps they're a bit like klingons. They just start fights all the time, but if you kill the ones who attack you, you gradually earn their respect. 
### The Lupins
I think the Lupins are more like classical werewolves. They enjoy hunting down humans. They spend a lot of time trying to assert domination over the others. I want the lupin to be matriarchal. The men and women are equally as strong, but it's easier for the lupin women to control the men in the pack. I want to turn the entire alpha male shit on it's head with them. I want to emphasize their gothic horror roots as well. Perhaps their area can be the old ruins of a former castle, now taken over.
##### Other Species
I'm also going to want to add several other species, the Lapin, Vulpin, Selkies, Satyr, and such. They probably won't have their own areas, but will appear regularly past the dark city, doing their own things.
