I'm starting to think about how I want to structure and theme the dungeon beyond the starting kobold/vermen area. My overall idea for the dungeon is an impossible castle like structure. I think I want the dungeon floors to extend upward, rather than down. The entrance of the dungeon can be in the side of a mountain, so having the dungeon proceed upward for a few floors seems logical, though as you climb a hundred floors it's clear that the space the dungeon occupies doesn't make any logical sense. I'll make this clear a few levels up, when some rooms randomly have windows that look out across impossible landscapes. The views that these random windows display become increasingly bizarre the further up you go.

##### The Lower Levels 
The lowest levels of the dungeon are the most traditional. Stone walls, areas that might have been prisons or jails; sewer areas, and caves. Havlins will sometimes show up in the lower levels, doing crimes. 
##### The Dark City
After you've managed to find a way past the lower levels you'll arrive in a 'city' type area. The city is still clearly entirely indoors, with the roof far overhead. As you proceed higher in the city the level design features more bridges that cross between buildings with the streets below. We'll need a way to adjust the dungeon builder so that it can cluster rooms together in islands that we then connect with bridges. The story of the city should focus on various gangs, fighting for territory, doing crimes.
