- The Church is the most powerful faction in the city. They maintain a low profile though so it's not obvious they exert influence over the other seemingly powerful factions.
- Officially the church is against exploring the dungeon. They tell people that they are free to delve given that their goal is to destroy the dungeon. 
- The reality though is that they're incredibly corrupt. They run the slavers guilds, who capture the dungeon monsters as slaves. The control the houses that then train the slaves, and distributes the trained slaves to the nobility who live lives of utter decadence. 
- They are most interested in maintaining this status quo.
##### Tenants of the Holy Church
- The dungeons are evil, sources of corruption that must be destroyed.
- The powers that the dungeons can give people (magic, abilities, etc) are a form of corruption.
- These two main tenants are in direct conflict as only those with power (granted by the dungeon) can hope to delve deeply into it.
- Humans are the only real sentient people, dungeon monsters are little more than puppets and don't really have feelings. They only act like they do.
- The church is against any form of magic, even healing and blessing. 
- Like the IRL churches, they view their God as all powerful, all knowing, etc, and like the real world, doesn't actually exist.
##### Clergy
- The clergy are split into multiple levels. At the lowest levels the priests and priestesses are told that the church is there to help people and protect humanity. They do the outward charitable work to build favor with the people. They actually do a good job, providing food, shelter, and healing; all the basic necessities for the poor.
- Though there is magical healing in the setting, the church is against it, so the clergy will mostly just ease people's suffering as they die, and bury them afterwards.
- Priests and priestesses who "show promise" are groomed by the upper levels of the clergy into eventually learning the true nature of the church. 
- There are secret sects within these middle layers that serve different purposes. Some are concubine like nuns, or assassins, or any other secret work the church can't be public about. 
- Though their official stance is no dungeon corruption the templars of the church are obviously enhanced with dungeon magic. They wear armor that they never remove. The church plays it off as the power of God or whatever. Inside of their armor the templars are terribly disfigured and monstrous.
- The church should have a branch called the choir, a secret cabal of sex wizards or some such.

### History of the Church
There's actually a kernel of truth behind the teachings of the church. The dungeon is evil and the powers that the adventurers gain by delving the dungeon are corrupting. Their goal, to destroy the dungeons, though is impossible, at least the way they're going about it. The church may have spent their early years actually pursuing this impossible goal. Eventually though, as it happens with every organization, only the worst people manage to get put in charge. The current leadership, like the nobility, only want to live lives of excess and depravity, which has led to its current state.

There will be a few mid-level people in the organization who see this situation for what it is though, and will hold true to the actual tenants of the church, having seen reality for what it is. There are books of church doctrine that explain how the dungeons have stymied the growth of humanity. A common complaint about fantasy fiction is that it doesn't make sense for the world to be forever stuck in whatever medieval technology level that has existed for tens of thousands of years. Technology and progress only happen though when people are doing something hard, and find an easier way to do things. The dungeons provide all of these easy solutions though magic, the catch being that magic is opinionated and as a gift from the dungeon, it can really only be used for what the dungeon itself allows. Also because the accumulation of power only happens though a very risky behavior, powerful people can be culled whenever they rise to the point where they might be a problem.

##### The Old Truth Seeing Priest
I don't have a name for this character yet, but I want to have someone the player can interact with that represents what the church actually should be. 
- He's an older man, stuck in an undesirable role because the church hierarchy wouldn't want a person like him to have any actual power in the organization, but can't actually get rid of the guy. 
- He wouldn't be in a teaching role either. He'd be a "disruptive" influences over the novices. 
- Perhaps something like an archivist. 
- So then why would the player need to interact with an old church archivist?
- The player could uncover a plot from the church higher ups to silence the man. Hell, the church might even hire the player to get rid of him. This would give the player the option of just killing the guy or advancing his story. Killing him would lead to complications with the Priestess and her story line though, so killing him should lead to a fast bad end, where the people who hired you to do the job kill you once you've completed it, rightly accusing you of his murder.
Once you've gained his trust he becomes and source of lore about the church and the world. He shouldn't exposition dump, but the player will have access to the archives where they can read books, Skyrim style, if they want extra information. He's also there to teach the Priestess about the true nature of the church, helping to influence her towards joining the player's faction.
